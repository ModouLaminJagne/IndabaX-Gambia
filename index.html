Mourad Telmini is a Professor of Physics at the University of Tunis El Manar and Director of the LSAMA lab. He specializes in quantum physics, ultrafast laser control, and quantum computing, and collaborates with international universities as a visiting researcher.

Speaker Name

Professor of Physics at University of Tunis El Manar

Mourad Telmini earned a PhD in Atomic Physics from the University of Paris-Sud (Paris-Saclay) in 1993. He has been President of the Tunisian Physical Society (2005–2007), member of the C13 commission of the International Union for Pure and Applied Physics (2006–2014), Director General of the National Center for Nuclear Science and Technology (2010–2012), and Vice-Chair of the African Commission on Nuclear Energy (2011–2014). Presently, he serves as Professor of Physics at the University of Tunis El Manar, Director of the Research Laboratory on Atomic and Molecular Spectroscopy & Applications (LSAMA), Coordinator of the Tunisian Quantum Network QUANTUN, and President of the Tunisian Metrology Association. He is a visiting professor and researcher in several universities (Paris-Saclay, Le Havre-Normandy, University of São Paulo, University of South Africa, and Purdue University). His main research topics include: Quantum Physics in atomic and molecular systems, especially Rydberg states; Coherent control of molecular processes with ultra-short laser pulses; Coherent transport of cold atoms and Bose-Einstein condensates in moving optical lattices; and Quantum computing for molecular systems.

Speaker Name

Senior Lecturer in Geospatial & AI for Earth Observation

HDR. Dr.-Ing. Nesrine Chehata, Senior Lecturer at Bordeaux INP, is an expert in geospatial technologies and artificial intelligence for Earth Observation and the environment. She has worked at CNES (the French Space Agency), IGN (the French National Mapping Agency), and IRD-Tunisia (French Research and Development Institute). She also serves as an expert for the European Space Agency’s Environment Group. Since 2016, she has co-founded and presided over AGEOS, the African Association for Geospatial Development, where she actively promotes geospatial technologies across Tunisia and Africa, building capacity through engagement with public institutions, local authorities, students, and children. A strong advocate for open geographic data, she has contributed to inclusive governance, innovation, and entrepreneurship by promoting the reuse of open geodata. She leads hackathons focused on open geodata, artificial intelligence, and climate change adaptation. She also supports startups and companies in the climate-oriented geospatial sector. Since 2021, she has served as President of the National Forum on Climate Change Adaptation (FNACC) and currently chairs the UNESCO Chair in Climate Change Adaptation at Bordeaux INP, France. Through these roles, she highlights the importance of space-based technologies in climate action and supports local initiatives to build sustainable and resilient African cities .
